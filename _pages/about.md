I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science and an IMPRS-IS scholar in the Neuromechanics and Rehabilitation Robotics Group at the University of Tübingen. My research focuses on uncertainty in machine learning and human intention prediction for reliable, human-centered assistive robotics.

Before starting my Ph.D., I completed an M.Sc. in Computer Science at Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT), specializing in software engineering and machine learning. My master's research at the German Aerospace Center (DLR) studied human-interpretable explanations of uncertainty in point cloud registration.

My broader interests include uncertainty quantification, safe learning-enabled robotic systems, visual-inertial odometry, and the translation of machine learning methods into clinical and assistive applications.
